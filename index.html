The Offset Problem

Why traditional carbon offsets
don't work for small businesses.

New environmental laws are coming to India. While big corporations have teams of experts to handle this, small businesses are left behind with no easy way to go green.

New Environmental Rules in India (CCTS)

The government is starting to track carbon emissions for huge factories (like cement, steel, and textiles). While large conglomerates get issued official carbon credits, small and medium businesses contribute over 30% of Indian industrial emissions and do not have access to these regulatory platforms, leaving them stuck with complicated, outdated, manual systems.

Authority: Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) & Ministry of Power Timeline: Rolling out in 2025 & 2026 Target: Clean Industrial Operations
Barrier 01

High Minimum Limits (10 Tonnes)

Traditional brokers only sell offsets in massive bulk orders. If your business emits just 350 kg, they force you to buy 10,000 kg (10 tonnes) or nothing at all.

Barrier 02

Hidden Fees & Markups

There is no public price list for carbon offsets. Middlemen buy cheap credits from rural projects and resell them with hidden markups of up to 45% to small business owners.

Barrier 03

Risk of Getting Cheated

Without a secure digital tracking system, brokers can sell the exact same carbon credit to multiple companies. This makes your green claims look fake and causes audit failures.

Absolute Beginner's Guide: What does "1 kg of Carbon" mean?
If you are new to carbon offsets, numbers like "1 kg of Carbon" can sound confusing. Here are real-world physical equivalents to help you visualize what 1 kg of carbon looks like:
🌳

Tree Planting

A mature tree must grow and absorb carbon for about 15 days to clean up exactly 1 kg of carbon from the air. Offsetting speeds up this process globally.

🚗

Driving a Car

Driving about 4.5 kilometers in a standard petrol car burns fuel and releases exactly 1 kg of carbon directly into the sky.

💡

Using Electricity

Running a standard home ceiling fan in India for 30 hours consumes electricity equal to 1 kg of carbon emissions.
